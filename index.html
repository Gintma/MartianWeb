The first Evil Martians open_source_font

Martian Mono  is a monospaced version of the Martian Grotesk  font for code style design.

It inherits Grotesk’s brutal and eye-catching aesthetics, as well as all of its benefits: metrics equilibrium, readability and intelligibility, and convenience for web developers and designers who believe in a systematic approach to design.

[
001
]
Aesthetics

Thanks to overhanging terminals, a closed aperture, and an almost complete lack of contrast, Martian Mono strikes a balance between readability and a strong, slightly brutal personality.

[
002
]
Variable

Looking for a condensed font for your terminal? Mono is one of the rare coding fonts that has two axes of variation: from thin to thick letters along the “Weight” axis, and from compressed to stretched letters along the “Width” axis from Condensed to Semi-Wide.

[
003
]
Dev Experience

The font’s vertical metrics make it a Swiss army knife for web developers. The calculations ensure equal space above the cap height and under the baseline, resulting in centered glyphs. This enables even placement of text labels on buttons, inputs, lists, and forms.

[
004
]
Legibility

Originally crafted for screens, the glyph heights adhere to the pixel grid on commonly used font sizes. Additionally, equipped with OpenType and TrueType hinting, Martian Mono remains legible on most platforms, even at small sizes.
